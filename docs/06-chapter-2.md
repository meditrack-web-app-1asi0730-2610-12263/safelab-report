# **Chapter II: Requirements Elicitation & Analysis**
## **2.1. Competitors**
### **2.1.1. Competitive Analysis**
**Why conduct this analysis?**
Identify the barriers to entry (technological and economic) imposed by global IoT monitoring leaders, in order to validate that there is an underserved niche in medium and small healthcare institutions. This analysis will allow us to position SafeLab as an agile, clinically specific, and economically accessible alternative.

<br>

| Attribute | SafeLab | SmartSense | SenseAnywhere | Monnit |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Overview** | B2B SaaS startup focused on eradicating biological waste through agile and accessible automation. | High-level corporate IoT platform for traceability and compliance in hospital and pharmaceutical networks. | European cloud monitoring system, focused on ultra-durable hardware and cold chain logistics. | Global provider of remote monitoring solutions with wireless sensors for multiple industries. |
| **Competitive Advantage** | Hardware-agnostic platform, highly contextualized to the workflow of biologists. | Massive scale capacity and integration with ERP systems and strict regulatory compliance (FDA). | Extreme hardware reliability and uninterrupted cloud logging without local maintenance. | Initial economic accessibility and extreme customization to monitor almost any variable. |
| **Target Market** | Clinical laboratories and hospital pharmacies in emerging or peripheral cities. | Large hospitals, national pharmacy chains, and global pharmaceutical logistics. | High-tech warehouses, pharmaceutical laboratories, and logistics transport companies. | Small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) in any sector (agriculture, IT, food, clinics). |
| **Marketing Strategies** | Inbound marketing focused on the "Zero Waste Culture" and the simplification of local quality audits. | Corporate B2B sales, focused on return on investment (ROI) through legal risk mitigation. | Presence at global pharmaceutical trade shows. | Massive digital marketing, direct e-commerce, and search engine positioning at a low cost. |
| **Products and Services** | Web Application SPA + API integration with generic and affordable third-party hardware. | Enterprise software + Gateways + Proprietary IoT sensors. | SenseAnywhere Cloud + AiroSensors (Closed proprietary hardware). | iMonnit Platform + ALTA wireless sensors. |
| **Prices and Costs** | Low. Purely SaaS model with monthly/annual payments, allowing the reuse of generic equipment. | Very high. Annual enterprise contracts that include expensive hardware and installation. | Medium-High. Depends on the import of their specialized European sensors. | Low-Medium. Affordable hardware and tiered monthly or annual subscriptions. |
| **Distribution Channels** | Direct B2B sales and self-onboarding. | Corporate direct sales. Web Platform and Mobile App. | Official distributor network. SaaS Web Platform. | Own online store and distributors. Web and Mobile Platform. |
| **Strengths** | High agility to pivot, low structural cost, and interface designed purely for the clinical user. | Unshakeable brand reputation and international certifications. | Market-leading hardware (10 years without charging) and highly stable software. | Vast catalog of sensors and highly customizable interface. |
| **Opportunities** | Existence of an immense market of medium-sized laboratories that use paper processes because they cannot afford large competitors. | Absorption of smaller competitors and government contracts. | Expansion in emerging markets and improvement of its API integration. | Constant growth in post-pandemic digitalization in medium-sized clinics. |
| **Weaknesses** | Lack of proprietary hardware and lack of brand recognition in the initial stage. | Inaccessible for small clinics. Requires slow corporate implementation processes. | Closed hardware model; if a sensor is damaged, another must be imported from the manufacturer. | Platform is too generic; it is not designed specifically for specific workflows. |
| **Threats** | Initial distrust of the healthcare sector towards new platforms or the entry of a tech giant into the low-cost market. | Emergence of agile and affordable startups in local markets. | Problems in global microchip supply chains that make their hardware more expensive. | Niche solutions that steal their healthcare sector clients by having more specialized interfaces. |
